Cloud Edge Trading

Use the Keltner Channel as dynamic support/resistance based on flow direction

The Core Concept

The Keltner Channel (the "cloud") acts as dynamic support and resistance. But here's the key insight: which edge matters depends on the flow direction.

When capital is flowing bullish (green cloud), the bottom of the cloud becomes support. When capital is flowing bearish (red cloud), the top of the cloud becomes resistance.

Long Setup
  • Flow is GREEN (bullish)
  • Price touches BOTTOM of cloud
  • Enter long on bounce
  • Target: Previous swing high or top of cloud
  • Stop: Below the cloud
Short Setup
  • Flow is RED (bearish)
  • Price touches TOP of cloud
  • Enter short on rejection
  • Target: Previous swing low or bottom of cloud
  • Stop: Above the cloud

Best Timeframes

This strategy works across all timeframes but shines on lower timeframes (1m-15m) for scalping:

  • 1m-5m: Quick scalps, multiple opportunities per session
  • 15m: Sweet spot‚Äîenough signals with cleaner moves
  • 1H: Fewer but larger moves, good for swing entries
  • 4H+: Use for bias, but fewer cloud edge touches

When to avoid this strategy: During low-volume periods (weekends, holidays), when the cloud is very thin, or when you see frequent flow flips within a short period. These conditions indicate indecision and increase whipsaw risk.

Entry Checklist

  1. Confirm flow direction ‚Äî Is the cloud green or red?
  2. Check cloud thickness ‚Äî Thick = good, thin = caution
  3. Wait for edge touch ‚Äî Price must reach the cloud edge
  4. Look for reaction ‚Äî A bounce (green) or rejection (red)
  5. Enter on confirmation candle ‚Äî Don't front-run the touch
  6. Set stop beyond cloud ‚Äî If price breaks through, you're wrong

Pro Tip: Combine this with higher timeframe bias. If the 1H flow is green and you're scalping the 5m, prioritize long setups off the bottom of the 5m cloud. Trading with the higher timeframe flow increases your win rate.

Dual Take-Profit System

Maximize gains with TP1 at middle band, TP2 at opposite band

The Strategy

Building on Cloud Edge Trading, this system uses two take-profit levels to lock in profits while letting winners run. The middle Keltner band (basis) acts as TP1, and the opposite outer band becomes TP2.

Long Trade Management
  • Entry: Bottom of Keltner (green flow)
  • Stop Loss: Below lower band (~1.5%)
  • TP1 (50%): Middle band (basis)
  • Move SL to breakeven after TP1
  • TP2 (50%): Upper band (opposite edge)
Short Trade Management
  • Entry: Top of Keltner (red flow)
  • Stop Loss: Above upper band (~1.5%)
  • TP1 (50%): Middle band (basis)
  • Move SL to breakeven after TP1
  • TP2 (50%): Lower band (opposite edge)

Why This Works

The Keltner Channel is a volatility-based envelope. Price naturally oscillates between the bands. By entering at one extreme and targeting the middle (TP1) then the opposite extreme (TP2), you're trading WITH the natural market rhythm.

The key insight: TP1 at the middle band is almost always hit because it's just a reversion to the mean. TP2 at the opposite band catches the extended moves when flow is strong.

Critical Rule: Always move your stop to breakeven after TP1 hits. This turns every trade into either a winner or a scratch. In our backtest, this rule protected Trade #5 from becoming a loss.

Position Sizing Example

With a $10,000 account risking 1.5% ($150) per trade:

  • Entry: Full position at cloud edge
  • TP1 (50%): Close half (+$125 on avg)
  • TP2 (50%): Close remaining (+$250 on avg)
  • Total avg profit: $375 per trade (2.5R)
  • If TP2 stopped at BE: Still +$125 (0.8R)

Best Timeframe: This system shines on 4H charts where flow direction is more sustained and the Keltner bands are wide enough to capture meaningful moves. Lower timeframes work but have more noise.
