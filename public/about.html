The Problem with Traditional Analysis

Most traders focus on price action alone. They draw lines, spot patterns, and try to predict where price will go next. But here's what they're missing: price follows money.

Before a big move happens, capital starts flowing. Institutions don't just wake up and buy ‚Äî they rotate capital from one asset class to another based on risk appetite, macro conditions, and sector strength. By the time price "breaks out," the smart money is already positioned.

What is Capital Flow?

Capital flow analysis tracks where money is moving across different asset classes and market sectors. Instead of just looking at Bitcoin's price, we ask: Where is capital rotating right now?

The Data Sources

  • DXY (Dollar Index) ‚Äî When the dollar weakens, risk assets like crypto typically benefit
  • VIX (Volatility Index) ‚Äî Low VIX = risk-on sentiment, high VIX = fear in the market
  • Gold ‚Äî Safe haven flows; money leaving gold often flows into risk assets
  • US 10Y Yields ‚Äî Rising yields can pressure risk assets; falling yields support them
  • BTC Flow ‚Äî Direct measurement of Bitcoin capital movement
  • ETH Flow ‚Äî Ethereum often leads altcoin rotations
  • BTC Dominance ‚Äî High dominance = majors favored; falling = alt season potential
  • TOTAL3 ‚Äî Total altcoin market cap excluding BTC/ETH; measures alt sector strength

Why This Matters for Your Trading

1. Trade with the Flow, Not Against It

When capital is flowing into risk assets across all sectors, fighting that tide is swimming upstream. The Capital Flow Filter helps you align your trades with institutional money movement.

2. Better Risk Management

Knowing the macro environment helps you size positions appropriately. Full confluence? Consider larger size. Mixed signals? Trade smaller or sit out.

3. Earlier Entries and Exits

Capital often starts rotating before price confirms. The indicator can give you a heads up that conditions are shifting, allowing you to position ahead of the crowd.

4. Avoid Manipulation Traps

The indicator includes manipulation detection ‚Äî identifying unusual volume patterns, wick hunting, and spoofing. When the market is playing games, you'll know.
