Methodology

Understanding why each input was chosen and how they work together to track capital rotation

The Core Philosophy

Capital Flow Filter is built on a simple premise: price follows money. Before crypto moves, capital rotates across macro assets, between risk-on and risk-off positions, and within crypto sectors.

By tracking these flows across 8 carefully selected inputs, we can identify the direction of capital rotation before it fully manifests in price. Each input was chosen based on:

  • Correlation ‚Äî Does it consistently lead or coincide with crypto moves?
  • Data availability ‚Äî Is it accessible on TradingView across timeframes?
  • Signal clarity ‚Äî Does it provide clear directional information?
  • Non-redundancy ‚Äî Does it add unique information vs other inputs?

Current Inputs (v4.5.1)

The indicator tracks 8 assets across 3 categories. Each is analyzed for rate-of-change across multiple timeframes, then weighted by its recent predictive accuracy.

Macro (4 inputs)
Traditional finance risk sentiment
DXY (Dollar Index) Risk-Off

Measures USD strength against a basket of major currencies. Dollar strength typically pressures risk assets including crypto.

‚Üì DXY falling = Bullish crypto ‚Üë DXY rising = Bearish crypto
VIX (Volatility Index) Risk-Off

The "fear gauge" for traditional markets. High VIX indicates market stress and risk-off sentiment that spills into crypto.

‚Üì VIX falling = Bullish crypto ‚Üë VIX spiking = Bearish crypto
Gold (XAUUSD) Risk-Off

Classic safe haven asset. When capital flows to gold, it often flows away from risk assets. Gold and BTC sometimes compete as "digital gold."

‚Üì Gold falling = Bullish crypto ‚Üë Gold rising = Bearish crypto
US10Y (10-Year Yields) Risk-Off

Rising yields increase the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like crypto. Yield spikes often precede risk-off moves.

‚Üì Yields falling = Bullish crypto ‚Üë Yields rising = Bearish crypto

BTC (Bitcoin) Leader

The market leader. BTC sets the overall crypto market direction. Institutional flows typically hit BTC first before rotating elsewhere.

‚Üë BTC rising = Bullish ‚Üì BTC falling = Bearish
ETH (Ethereum) Leader

The second leader and DeFi backbone. ETH strength often precedes altcoin rotation. ETH/BTC ratio is a key risk-on indicator.

Sector Rotation (2 inputs)
Where is capital sitting within crypto?
BTC.D (BTC Dominance) Rotation

Percentage of total crypto market cap in BTC. Rising dominance = flight to safety. Falling dominance = capital rotating to alts.

‚Üì BTC.D falling = Bullish alts ‚Üë BTC.D rising = Risk-off / BTC only
TOTAL3 (Alt Market Cap) Rotation

Total altcoin market cap excluding BTC and ETH. Direct measure of capital in the alt sector. Rising TOTAL3 = alt season conditions.

‚Üë TOTAL3 rising = Bullish alts ‚Üì TOTAL3 falling = Bearish alts

Input Evaluation Framework

When evaluating new inputs, we ask:

  • Predictive value: Does it lead crypto price action or just coincide?
  • Consistency: Does it work across different market regimes?
  • Timeframe stability: Is it reliable on both scalp and swing timeframes?
  • Data quality: Is it available on TradingView with clean, continuous data?
  • Uniqueness: Does it add information not captured by existing inputs?
  • Interpretability: Is the signal logic clear and explainable?
